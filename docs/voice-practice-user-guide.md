## Section 1: Getting Started with Voice Practice

### What is Voice Practice?

Voice Practice is an interactive way to improve your social skills by having real conversations with an AI coach. Instead of reading text or clicking buttons, you **speak** just like you would in real life, making it feel more natural and authentic.

### How It Helps Improve Social Skills

- **Real Conversation Practice**: Talk naturally instead of just thinking about what to say
- **Safe Environment**: Practice without worrying about judgment from others
- **Immediate Feedback**: Get helpful guidance right after each exchange
- **Build Confidence**: The more you practice, the more comfortable you'll feel
- **Learn from Mistakes**: The AI gently helps you improve your responses

### What to Expect from the AI Coach

Our AI coach, **Cue**, is designed to be:
- **Patient and Encouraging**: No pressure, take your time
- **Age-Appropriate**: Uses language that matches your grade level
- **Supportive**: Celebrates your efforts and progress
- **Helpful**: Provides gentle guidance when you need it

### Typical Session Length

A complete voice practice session typically takes **3-5 minutes**. This includes:
- Introduction and scenario setup
- 5-8 conversation exchanges
- Final feedback and encouragement

## Section 2: How to Start Voice Practice

Follow these simple steps to begin your first voice practice session:

### Step 1: Navigate to Voice Practice

- Click the **"Voice Practice"** card on the Practice tab
- Or click the **üé§ Voice** button on any scenario card

### Step 2: Select a Scenario

- Browse available scenarios by category
- Choose one that matches your interests or goals
- Each scenario shows:
  - **Difficulty level** (Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced)
  - **Estimated duration** (usually 3-5 minutes)
  - **Description** of what you'll practice

### Step 3: Allow Microphone Access

When you click "Start Practice," your browser will ask for microphone permission:

- **Click "Allow"** or **"Permit"** to continue
- You'll only need to do this once per browser
- If you accidentally blocked it, check your browser settings

### Step 4: Wait for Connection

- You'll see a **"Connecting..."** message
- Wait until you see **"Connected - Start speaking!"**
- This usually takes just a few seconds

### Step 5: Start Speaking

- When you see **"Listening..."** in green, the AI is ready to hear you
- **Speak naturally** - just like talking to a friend
- The AI will respond after you finish speaking

## Section 5: Troubleshooting Common Issues

Having trouble? Here are solutions to common problems:

### Problem: Microphone Not Working

**Symptoms:**
- Browser isn't asking for microphone permission
- Status shows "Disconnected"
- Can't hear yourself speaking

**Solutions:**
1. **Check browser permissions**:
   - Look for a microphone icon in your browser's address bar
   - Click it and select "Allow"
   - Refresh the page if needed

2. **Check your device**:
   - Make sure your microphone is plugged in (if using external mic)
   - Check that your device's built-in mic is enabled
   - Try speaking to see if your mic picks up sound

3. **Check system settings**:
   - Go to your device's sound/system settings
   - Ensure microphone access is enabled for your browser
   - On Mac: System Preferences ‚Üí Security & Privacy ‚Üí Microphone
   - On Windows: Settings ‚Üí Privacy ‚Üí Microphone

4. **Try refreshing**:
   - Refresh the page
   - Close and reopen your browser
   - Clear browser cache if problem persists

### Problem: Connection Fails

**Symptoms:**
- Stuck on "Connecting..." message
- Error message appears
- Can't start the session

**Solutions:**
1. **Check your internet connection**:
   - Make sure you're connected to Wi-Fi or data
   - Try loading another website to test connectivity
   - If on mobile, check your signal strength

2. **Refresh the page**:
   - Click the refresh button
   - Wait a few seconds and try again

3. **Try text practice instead**:
   - If voice practice isn't working, you can still practice with text
   - Click "Text Practice" instead of "Voice Practice"
   - You'll get the same learning benefits!

### Problem: Browser Not Supported

**Symptoms:**
- Error message about browser compatibility
- Features not working properly
- Audio issues

**Solutions:**
1. **Update your browser**:
   - Voice practice works best in **Chrome**, **Edge**, or **Safari**
   - Update to the latest version
   - Chrome: Settings ‚Üí About Chrome
   - Safari: App Store ‚Üí Updates
   - Edge: Settings ‚Üí About Microsoft Edge

2. **Mobile browsers**:
   - **iOS**: Use Safari (the default browser)
   - **Android**: Use Chrome browser
   - Make sure your mobile browser is up to date

3. **Alternative browsers**:
   - If your browser doesn't support voice features, try Chrome or Edge
   - These browsers have the best voice recognition support

### Problem: AI Not Understanding Me

**Symptoms:**
- Your words aren't being transcribed correctly
- AI responds to something you didn't say
- Transcription shows errors

**Solutions:**
1. **Speak more clearly**:
   - Enunciate your words
   - Speak at a moderate pace (not too fast or slow)
   - Pronounce words fully

2. **Reduce background noise**:
   - Move to a quieter location
   - Close windows and doors
   - Turn off music or TV

3. **Check microphone position**:
   - Keep microphone about 6-12 inches from your mouth
   - Speak directly into the microphone
   - Avoid covering the microphone

4. **Language**:
   - Make sure you're speaking **English**
   - The AI currently supports English conversations
   - Speak naturally - don't change your accent

### Problem: Audio Quality is Poor

**Symptoms:**
- Echo or feedback
- Muffled or unclear audio
- Interruptions or cutting out

**Solutions:**
1. **Use headphones or earbuds**:
   - This prevents echo from speakers
   - Improves audio clarity
   - Reduces feedback loops

2. **Check microphone quality**:
   - Built-in mics on phones/tablets work well
   - External USB mics may provide better quality
   - Avoid using mics far from your device

3. **Reduce background noise**:
   - Close windows and doors
   - Turn off fans or air conditioning if possible
   - Find a quiet room

4. **Move closer to microphone**:
   - Position yourself 6-12 inches away
   - Speak directly toward the mic
   - Avoid having objects between you and the mic

## Section 8: Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How long does a voice practice session take?

**A:** Typically **3-5 minutes** for a complete conversation. This includes the introduction, 5-8 exchanges, and final feedback. You can end early if needed.

### Q: Can I practice the same scenario multiple times?

**A:** Yes! **Repetition helps build confidence and skills**. Practice the same scenario as many times as you want. Each time you'll get more comfortable and improve your responses.

### Q: What if I'm shy or nervous?

**A:** The AI is **patient and encouraging**. Start with easier scenarios (marked as "Beginner") and work your way up. Remember:
- There's no pressure
- The AI is here to help, not judge
- Everyone gets nervous - that's normal!
- Practice helps reduce nervousness over time

### Q: Do I need headphones?

**A:** **Recommended but not required**. Headphones improve audio quality and prevent echo, especially if using speakers. If you don't have headphones, you can still practice - just make sure your speakers aren't too loud.

### Q: Can I use voice practice on my phone?

**A:** Yes! Voice practice works on:
- **iOS devices** - Use Safari browser (the default browser)
- **Android devices** - Use Chrome browser
- Make sure your mobile browser is up to date

### Q: What if I make a mistake?

**A:** **That's okay!** Making mistakes is how we learn. The AI will:
- Gently guide you toward better responses
- Not make you feel bad about mistakes
- Help you understand what could be improved
- Celebrate when you get it right

### Q: Can I switch to text practice mid-session?

**A:** Yes! You can **end voice practice anytime** and continue with text practice instead. Your progress is saved, and you can come back to voice practice later.

### Q: Is my voice recorded?

**A:** **No permanent recording**. Your audio is:
- Used **only in real-time** for transcription
- **Not permanently stored** as audio files
- **Processed and then discarded** immediately
- Only text transcripts may be saved for your progress tracking

### Q: What if I want to take a break during practice?

**A:** You can **pause anytime** by simply not speaking. The AI will wait patiently. Or you can:
- Click the back button to end the session
- Close the browser tab
- Your progress up to that point is saved

### Q: Can I practice with friends or family?

**A:** Voice practice is designed for **individual practice**, but you can:
- Practice together by taking turns
- Discuss scenarios before practicing
- Share what you learned after practice
- Encourage each other's progress

### Q: What scenarios are available?

**A:** There are many scenarios covering different social situations:
- Starting conversations
- Active listening
- Joining groups
- Handling disagreements
- Making plans
- Showing empathy
- Asking for help
- And more!

Scenarios are organized by **category** and **difficulty level**.

### Q: How do I know if I'm improving?

**A:** Check your **Progress screen** to see:
- Number of sessions completed
- Scenarios you've mastered
- Your conversation history
- Improvement trends over time

### Q: What if the AI doesn't understand what I'm saying?

**A:** Try these tips:
- Speak more clearly and slowly
- Reduce background noise
- Move closer to your microphone
- Make sure you're speaking English
- Check your microphone settings

### Q: Can I use voice practice offline?

**A:** No, voice practice requires an **internet connection** because it uses:
- Real-time speech recognition
- AI conversation features
- Audio processing

If you don't have internet, try **text practice** instead - it works offline after the initial load.
